Growth and Volatility of Micropolitan Statistical Areas in the U.S.

The objectives of this study are: (1) to describe the geographic distribution, growth, and volatility of the U.S. micropolitan areas during the 1969-2012 period; and (2) to identify and measure the determinants of growth and volatility of micropolitan areas.

The Role of the Housing Supply and the Regulatory Environment in Economic Growth of Micropolitan Statistical Areas

The purpose of this study is to identify and measure the impact of various factors that affect the economic performance of micropolitan statistical areas in the U.S. Specifically, it is hypothesized that micropolitan area economic growth is highly related to locational amenities, primarily the housing supply and the regulatory environment.


U.S. Micropolitan Area Growth: A Spatial Equilibrium Growth Analysis

This paper examines micropolitan area growth during the 1990s, a period of strong national growth. A spatial equilibrium growth framework and estimated reduced-form regressions containing an extensive number of variables are used to assess the sources of differentials in micropolitan area growth. .


The Impact Of SBA Lending Activity On Micropolitan Statistical Areas In The U.S. Southeast

This study examines the economic impact of Small Business Administration (SBA) guaranteed lending activity on the 12 states comprising the Southeast region (Alabama, Arkansas, Florida, Georgia, Kentucky, Louisiana, Mississippi, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, Virginia, and West Virginia) for the 1990-2015 period.
